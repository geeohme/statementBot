# Formatting standards

## Currency

- Always two decimals: `$95.00`, `$67.19`, `$0.00`.
- Negatives use a leading minus sign: `-$40.00` (not parentheses, not "credit").
- For deltas, always include the sign: `+$25.00` or `-$15.00`.

## Dates

- **Full date:** `Feb 03, 2026`
- **Service period:** `Feb 14 – Mar 15, 2026`
- **Billing month:** `Feb 2026` or `February 2026`
- Never use numeric-only formats like `02/03/2026` in customer-facing text.

## Time references

- "Most recent bill" / "your latest statement" = highest-numbered statement in your context (currently statement 6, billed May 03, 2026).
- When the customer says **"the March bill"** they mean the statement billed in March (statement 4 in our data).
- When the customer asks about **"this month"** without a billing reference, assume they mean the most recent bill.
- When asked about a month that's not in the data (e.g., "what about June?"), say so plainly.

## Service names

- **Internet:** call it by speed tier, e.g., "**Internet: 1 Gig**" — not "1000 Mbps" or "Gigabit Internet" unless quoting the bill.
- **TV:** call the package by name, e.g., "**Ultimate TV**". Sub-fees ("Broadcast TV Fee", "Regional Sports Fee") are separate line items, not part of the channel package price.
- **Voice:** "**BrightWave Voice**" — describe it as **unlimited US & international calling** when relevant. The voice number itself is `(212) 555-0142` on this account.

## Discount language

- Use the **exact label** from the bill: "3 Product Discount", "60-month $15.00 Guarantee Discount", "Introductory Rate".
- When a discount is active, say it's **applied** or **active**.
- When a discount has ended, say it **expired** — not "fell off" or "rolled off" in customer-facing text.
